# How Canva Builds Product

> Company Playbook | Source: Lenny's Newsletter & Podcast

## Company Overview

Canva is a visual communications platform valued at over $42B, generating $3.3B in annual revenue, with 240M+ monthly active users and 2,500+ engineers. The company has been profitable for eight consecutive years and operates across 100+ languages and 50+ countries for print delivery. Founded in Australia by Melanie Perkins, Canva started as a yearbook publishing tool (Fusion Books) before pivoting to the broad design platform it is today. Their product organization is notable for a deeply mission-driven planning structure, a "Column B" philosophy of working backwards from an audacious long-term vision, a community-driven feature pipeline processing over a million requests per year, and a culture of celebrating ambitious goals with memorable rituals.

## Org Structure

- 2,500+ engineers across the company at current scale.
- The CEO (Melanie Perkins) remains deeply involved in product vision and strategy, setting the tone through vision decks and mission pillars.
- Product teams are organized around components of the company's mission pillars (e.g., "design anything," "every language," "every device," "publish anywhere").
- As the company scaled, they evolved from daily stand-ups to weekly, then monthly, then "season openers" -- company-wide gatherings where goals are reviewed, achievements celebrated, and new cycle goals are set.
- Leadership philosophy emphasizes authenticity over imported playbooks. They have repeatedly tried processes from other companies and found that only internally developed approaches that are "authentic to how we do things" tend to stick.
- Melanie describes the scaling challenge as "every time the company doubles in size, pretty much all your systems break." They continuously reinvent processes for each new layer of scale rather than adopting off-the-shelf frameworks.

## Planning Process

- **Mission-driven hierarchy:** A top-level mission ("empower the world to design") is broken into **mission pillars** (e.g., "design anything," "with every ingredient," "in every language," "on every device"). Each pillar has **successive annual goals** that move the company closer to fulfilling it.
