# How Ramp Builds Product

> Company Playbook | Source: Lenny's Newsletter & Podcast

## Company Overview

Ramp provides corporate cards and spend management, helping companies save time and money. Valued at over $8 billion, Ramp is arguably the fastest-growing SaaS company of all time, hitting $100 million ARR in just two years and growing 4x to over $10 billion in annualized spending. What makes Ramp's product org remarkable is its obsessive culture of velocity: fewer than 5 PMs and 50 engineers shipped the product to $100M ARR. Under VP of Product Geoff Charles, every process, org decision, and hiring choice is optimized for speed of execution.

## Org Structure

- **PM, Design, Engineering, and Data all report to the CTO.** Having one tech-minded leader over all functions forces cooperation and prevents Product from becoming a "stakeholder" to Engineering rather than a "co-founder."
- **14 PMs** at time of profiling (~500+ employees). PM hiring is deliberately restricted until product is the obvious bottleneck to velocity.
- **Teams organized around business outcomes.** Each pod owns an accountable, measurable business goal (e.g., drive 50% of sales qualified leads via automated outbound, build a Bill.com competitor in three months).
- **Each pod has embedded cross-functional support:** dedicated Product Marketer, Product Operator, Product Partnerships Lead, and Product Counsel mapped to the tech team.
- **Small teams (5-10 engineers max)** that can split into sub-pods as they grow. Pods spin out once they have proven wins and need to work across teams.
- **Flexible staffing regardless of reporting lines.** People move between teams based on business needs. Management (hiring, coaching, development) stays stable while pod composition shifts.
- **Platform teams stay embedded** in core product teams as long as possible to maintain business context. They spin out only after multiple wins prove they need to serve multiple teams.
