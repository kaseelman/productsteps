# How Coda Builds Product

> Company Playbook | Source: Lenny's Newsletter & Podcast

## Company Overview

Coda is an all-in-one doc platform that combines documents, spreadsheets, and applications into a single surface. Founded by Shishir Mehrotra (former VP of Product & Engineering at YouTube/Google, Spotify board member), the company has built one of the most deliberate, first-principled product cultures in tech. With approximately 15 PMs and a joint Product and Design organization led by CPO Lane Shackleton, Coda is known for its obsessive approach to team rituals, its "Right vs. Familiar" value system, and its practice of dogfooding their own product for virtually every internal workflow.

## Org Structure

Coda operates a joint Product and Design team -- a deliberate choice born from Lane's observation at Google/YouTube that PMs became de facto decision-makers not through intent but because they accumulated the most context. By unifying the teams, PMs and designers share the same context and information, leading to more fluid roles. Sometimes designers write specs; sometimes PMs draw in Figma.

Structure: PMs report to PM leads, and both PM and Design leads report to CPO Lane Shackleton, who reports to CEO Shishir Mehrotra. Teams are structured around outcomes (e.g., a team focused on driving revenue mixing PDE and Customer roles). Within broader teams, "Investment Areas" (IAs) focus on specific journeys like onboarding or long-term outcomes like ecosystem growth.

Small teams with autonomy are the operating unit: roughly 1 PM, 1 designer, and 2-4 engineers, sometimes with Data and Insights support. The team composition is roughly 15 PMs and 30+ engineers. Team structure iterates about once per year, but core elements have remained consistent.
